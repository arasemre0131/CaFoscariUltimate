PART B: SHORT ANSWERS

1. A participle clause is a grammatical structure that uses a present or past participle (the -ing or -ed form of a verb) to modify a noun or pronoun in a sentence. For example: "Having completed the software update, the IT team monitored the system for any issues."

2. Defining relative clauses provide essential information about the noun they modify, while non-defining relative clauses add extra, non-essential details. For instance, "The laptop that was stolen from the office contained sensitive data" is a defining clause, while "The laptop, which was worth $1,500, was stolen from the office" is a non-defining clause. Non-defining clauses are often used to provide additional context or clarification in technical writing.

3. Modal verbs can help improve the clarity and precision of technical writing in computer science by:
- Expressing ability (can, could) - "The new software application can generate detailed reports."
- Indicating permission (can, may) - "Users may access the database with their assigned credentials."
- Conveying obligation (must, should) - "Employees must follow the company's cybersecurity protocols."
- Suggesting possibility (might, may) - "The machine learning algorithm might identify patterns in the data."

1. Active voice vs. passive voice in technical writing:
Active voice:
- Directly states the subject performing the action
- Typically more concise and engaging
- Helps establish a clear sense of responsibility and accountability
- Example: "The IT team installed the new firewall."

Passive voice:
- Focuses on the action rather than the subject
- Can be more formal and impersonal
- Useful for emphasizing the action or result rather than the performer
- Example: "The new firewall was installed."

Advantages of active voice:
- Clearer, more direct communication
- Easier for readers to follow and understand
- Conveys a stronger sense of agency and ownership

Advantages of passive voice:
- Allows the writer to be more objective and detached
- Helpful for emphasizing the process or outcome rather than the performer
- Can be appropriate for certain types of technical documentation, such as instructions or procedures

The choice between active and passive voice should depend on the specific context, audience, and communication goals of the technical document. A balanced approach that utilizes both styles strategically can create the most effective technical writing.
