Short Answers:
1. The modulus of a complex number z = a + bi is defined as |z| = √(a² + b²). The argument of z is the angle θ between the positive real axis and the line segment connecting the origin to the point (a, b) on the complex plane, such that tan(θ) = b/a. Complex numbers can be represented in polar form as z = |z|(cos(θ) + i sin(θ)).
2. The set of all n×n matrices forms a vector space under the operations of matrix addition and scalar multiplication because the set satisfies the 10 vector space axioms.
3. The rank-nullity theorem states that for any linear transformation T: V → W, the dimension of the domain V is equal to the sum of the dimension of the image (or range) of T and the dimension of the kernel (or null space) of T. This theorem is significant because it relates the dimensions of the key subspaces associated with a linear transformation.
4. The eigenvalues of A = [1 2; 3 4] are λ₁ = 5 and λ₂ = 0. The corresponding eigenvectors are v₁ = [2, 3]ᵀ and v₂ = [-2, 1]ᵀ.
5. Using Cramer's rule, the solution to the system 2x + 3y = 11, x - y = 5 is x = 3 and y = 2.
6. To change the basis of a linear transformation T: V → W from basis B to basis C, we need to find the change of basis matrix P such that T_C = P^-1 * T_B * P. The change of basis formula is significant because it allows us to represent the same linear transformation in different coordinate systems, which is important for computational and theoretical purposes.
