Limits are fundamental in calculus as they are used to define the concepts of continuity and differentiability, which are essential for understanding the behavior of functions and their applications.

2. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus states that the process of differentiation and integration are inverse operations. Specifically, it establishes a connection between the derivative of a function and the integral of that function, allowing us to compute integrals by finding antiderivatives. This theorem is the foundation for many applications of integral calculus.

3. Continuous functions are those for which small changes in the input result in small changes in the output. The properties of continuous functions include: (1) the function value at a point is equal to the limit of the function as x approaches that point, and (2) the function takes on all values between any two function values. An example of a function that is not continuous is the absolute value function |x|, which has a discontinuity at x = 0.

PART C: ESSAY QUESTIONS

1. Calculus is a powerful tool for modeling and understanding real-world phenomena across various disciplines. In physics, calculus is used to study the motion of objects, the dynamics of fluids, and the behavior of electromagnetic fields. For example, the equations of motion in classical mechanics can be derived using differentiation and integration, allowing us to predict the trajectories of projectiles or the orbits of planets. In economics, calculus is used to model and optimize decision-making processes, such as determining the optimal production quantity to maximize profit or analyzing the dynamics of supply and demand. Integral calculus, in particular, is used to calculate consumer and producer surplus, which are important measures of economic welfare. In biology, calculus is employed to model the growth and spread of populations, the dynamics of chemical reactions, and the transport of nutrients and resources within living organisms. For instance, the logistic growth model, which describes the population growth of a species limited by resources, can be derived using differential equations.

2. Continuity and differentiability are related but distinct concepts in calculus. A function is continuous at a point if the limit of the function as x approaches that point exists and is equal to the function value at that point. Differentiability, on the other hand, requires the function to be continuous and to have a well-defined derivative at that point. A function can be continuous but not differentiable at a point, such as the absolute value function |x|, which has a corner at x = 0 and is not differentiable at that point, even though it is continuous. The implications of a function being continuous but not differentiable at a point include the inability to apply techniques and theorems that rely on differentiability, such as the Mean Value Theorem and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. This can lead to complications in analyzing the behavior of the function and its applications.

3. Taylor series expansions are powerful mathematical tools that allow us to approximate functions using polynomial expressions. These approximations are particularly useful in science and engineering, where they enable us to solve complex problems and make predictions about the behavior of systems. For example, in physics, Taylor series expansions are used to linearize nonlinear equations, allowing for the application of analytical techniques. This is crucial in the study of dynamical systems, where the behavior near equilibrium points can be analyzed using the Taylor series approximation of the governing equations. In engineering, Taylor series expansions are employed in numerical methods, such as finite difference approximations, to discretize and solve partial differential equations that arise in the modeling of physical phenomena. Additionally, Taylor series expansions are fundamental to the field of machine learning, where they are used to optimize objective functions and develop efficient algorithms. However, it is important to note that Taylor series approximations have limitations and assumptions, such as the requirement of sufficient differentiability of the function and the convergence of the series within a specific domain. Careful consideration of these factors is necessary when applying Taylor series expansions in practice.
