2. The Mean Value Theorem states that if a function f(x) is continuous on a closed interval [a, b], then there exists at least one point c in (a, b) such that f'(c) = (f(b) - f(a))/(b - a). This allows us to analyze the average rate of change of a function over an interval and connect it to the derivative at some point.
