WEEK 1: Foundation Building

Day 1 (1.5-2 hours):
- Objectives: Understand the basic definitions of functions, domain, range, and properties of functions.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 1.1-1.3, lecture slides, online videos.
- Exercises: Practice identifying function types, finding domain and range, and sketching basic function graphs.
- Self-check: Can you determine if a given relation is a function? Can you find the domain and range of simple polynomial, rational, and trigonometric functions?

Day 2 (1.5-2 hours): 
- Objectives: Learn the concept of limits and how to evaluate limits of functions.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 2.1-2.3, lecture notes, online interactive simulations.
- Exercises: Compute limits of functions using various techniques (direct substitution, factoring, rationalization).
- Self-check: Can you find the limits of functions as x approaches a specific value? Can you determine when a limit does not exist?

Day 3 (1.5-2 hours):
- Objectives: Understand the definition of continuity and classify functions as continuous or discontinuous.
- Resources: Textbook chapter 2.4, lecture slides, online quizzes.
- Exercises: Analyze the continuity of functions at specific points and over intervals.
- Self-check: Can you identify the types of discontinuities (removable, jump, infinite) in a given function? Can you apply the Intermediate Value Theorem?

Day 4 (1.5-2 hours):
- Objectives: Introduction to differentiation and learn basic derivative rules.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 3.1-3.3, lecture videos, interactive derivative calculators.
- Exercises: Compute derivatives of polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions.
- Self-check: Can you find the derivative of a function using the power rule, constant rule, sum/difference rule, and product/quotient rule?

Day 5 (1.5-2 hours):
- Objectives: Continue exploring more advanced derivative rules and applications.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 3.4-3.6, lecture notes, online problem sets.
- Exercises: Practice applying the chain rule, implicit differentiation, and related rates problems.
- Self-check: Can you differentiate composite functions using the chain rule? Can you find the derivatives of inverse functions?

WEEK 2: Intermediate Development

Day 6 (2-2.5 hours):
- Objectives: Understand the concept of differentials and their applications.
- Resources: Textbook chapter 3.7, lecture slides, interactive demos.
- Exercises: Solve optimization problems using differentials and the MVT.
- Self-check: Can you use differentials to approximate function changes? Can you apply the Mean Value Theorem to analyze function behavior?

Day 7 (2-2.5 hours):
- Objectives: Introduction to integration and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 4.1-4.3, lecture videos, online integrators.
- Exercises: Practice indefinite and definite integral computations.
- Self-check: Can you use the Fundamental Theorem to evaluate definite integrals? Can you find the antiderivative of basic functions?

Day 8 (2-2.5 hours):
- Objectives: Explore various integration techniques, including substitution and integration by parts.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 4.4-4.6, lecture notes, online problem sets.
- Exercises: Apply substitution and integration by parts to compute integrals of more complex functions.
- Self-check: Can you choose the appropriate integration technique for a given integral? Can you set up and execute substitutions and integration by parts correctly?

Day 9 (2-2.5 hours):
- Objectives: Learn about improper integrals and their convergence/divergence.
- Resources: Textbook chapter 4.7, lecture slides, online interactive visualizations.
- Exercises: Evaluate improper integrals and determine their convergence or divergence.
- Self-check: Can you identify the conditions for the convergence of improper integrals? Can you use the comparison test to analyze convergence?

Day 10 (2-2.5 hours):
- Objectives: Introduction to sequences and series, including the concept of convergence.
- Resources: Textbook chapters 11.1-11.3, lecture videos, online quizzes.
- Exercises: Determine the convergence or divergence of basic sequences and series.
- Self-check: Can you distinguish between convergent and divergent sequences and series? Can you apply the basic convergence tests (comparison, ratio, root)?
